**Project Overview**
I built a Markov model to compare Standard Therapy vs. a hypothetical New Therapy for Type 2 Diabetes. The model calculates costs, QALYs, and ICER over a 20-year time horizon.

**Key components included:**

Health States: Controlled, Complication, Death

Transition Matrix: Simulates patient movement between health states

Markov Trace: Tracks cohort distribution over cycles

Cost & QALY Calculation: Computes total costs and quality-adjusted life years

Incremental Cost-Effectiveness Ratio (ICER): Evaluates cost per additional QALY
Learning Objectives

Understand Markov modelling structure and transitions

Apply cost and utility calculations to simulate outcomes

Interpret cost-effectiveness results and ICER

Build foundation for advanced HEOR models including sensitivity analyses and probabilistic simulations

**How to Use**

Open the Excel file Type2Diabetes_HEOR_Model.xlsx

**Explore sheets:**

*Inputs*: Parameter values for costs, utilities, and transition probabilities

*Transition Matrix*: Probabilities for moving between health states

*Markov Trace*: Cohort distribution over cycles

*Cost_QALY*: Cost and QALY calculations per cycle

*ICER*: Incremental cost-effectiveness analysis

Adjust parameters to see how costs and QALYs change (learning exercise)
